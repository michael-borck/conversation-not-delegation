# RTCF Prompt Framework
## Structured Approach to AI Tool Usage

**Module:** Week 1 - Introduction to AI in Business
**Duration:** 15 minutes (pre-lab) + integrated into workshop activities
**Purpose:** Establish foundational skills for effective AI tool interaction

---

## Overview

The RTCF Framework provides a structured approach to crafting effective prompts for AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Copilot. Mastering this framework will improve the quality of AI outputs throughout this unit and in your professional practice.

---

## The RTCF Framework

| Component | Purpose | Key Question |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| **R**ole | Define the AI's expertise/persona | "Who should the AI be?" |
| **T**ask | Specify what you want done | "What should it do?" |
| **C**ontext | Provide relevant background | "What does it need to know?" |
| **F**ormat | Describe desired output | "How should it look?" |

---

## Component Details

### R - Role

Assign the AI a relevant expert persona to focus its responses.

**Examples:**
- "You are a business analyst specialising in telecommunications..."
- "Act as an experienced project manager in the technology sector..."
- "You are a data scientist explaining concepts to non-technical executives..."

**Why it matters:** Role-setting activates relevant knowledge domains and adjusts communication style.

---

### T - Task

Clearly state the specific action you want the AI to perform.

**Weak Task:** "Tell me about customer churn"
**Strong Task:** "Identify the top 5 factors that cause customer churn in telecommunications companies and explain each in 2-3 sentences"

**Task Verbs to Use:**
- Analyse, Compare, Summarise, Explain
- Create, Draft, Generate, Develop
- Evaluate, Critique, Recommend, Prioritise
- List, Outline, Structure, Organise

---

### C - Context

Provide background information the AI needs to give relevant responses.

**Context Elements:**
- Industry or domain specifics
- Organisational constraints
- Target audience characteristics
- Relevant data or examples
- Prior decisions or requirements

**Example:** "Cloudcore is a mid-sized cloud services provider with 500 employees across Sydney, Singapore, and San Francisco. They are experiencing 50% year-over-year growth and facing challenges with knowledge silos between sales and support teams."

---

### F - Format

Specify how you want the output structured and presented.

**Format Options:**
- Bullet points vs. paragraphs
- Tables with specific columns
- Step-by-step numbered lists
- Word/length limits
- Professional tone vs. casual
- Include/exclude specific elements

**Example:** "Present your response as a table with columns: Challenge, AI Solution, Expected Benefit, Implementation Complexity (High/Medium/Low)"

## RTCF in Action: Examples

### Example 1: Business Analysis Task

**Without RTCF:**
> "What AI can Cloudcore use?"

**With RTCF:**
> **Role:** You are a technology consultant specialising in AI strategy for mid-sized enterprises.
>
> **Task:** Recommend 3 AI applications that would address Cloudcore's operational challenges.
>
> **Context:** Cloudcore is a cloud services provider with 500 employees, experiencing rapid growth but struggling with knowledge silos between departments and inconsistent customer support quality across regions.
>
> **Format:** Present each recommendation in this structure: (1) AI Application Name, (2) Problem Addressed, (3) Expected Benefits, (4) Implementation Complexity. Limit each recommendation to 100 words.

---

### Example 2: Data Analysis Support

**Without RTCF:**
> "Help me with this data"

**With RTCF:**
> **Role:** You are a data analyst helping a business student understand customer behaviour patterns.
>
> **Task:** Explain how to interpret customer segmentation results from a clustering analysis.
>
> **Context:** I've used Orange Data Mining to cluster customers into 4 groups based on purchase frequency, average order value, and support ticket volume. I need to present findings to a non-technical audience.
>
> **Format:** Provide (1) a name for each cluster type, (2) 2-3 characteristics that define each cluster, (3) one business recommendation per cluster. Use simple language without technical jargon.

---

### Example 3: Report Writing Support

**Without RTCF:**
> "Write about AI ethics"

**With RTCF:**
> **Role:** You are assisting an IS student preparing a professional consultant report.
>
> **Task:** Draft a 200-word section on ethical considerations for implementing AI in customer service.
>
> **Context:** The client is an Australian financial services company. The AI will handle initial customer inquiries and route complex issues to human agents. Key concerns include bias, transparency, and regulatory compliance with Australian privacy laws.
>
> **Format:** Professional business writing style with clear topic sentence, 3-4 supporting points, and a concluding recommendation. Reference Australian AI Ethics Principles where relevant.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Structure improves quality** - Random prompts get random results
2. **Context is critical** - AI can't read your mind; provide background
3. **Format saves time** - Specify what you want upfront
4. **Iteration is normal** - Refine prompts based on initial outputs
5. **Document your process** - Your AI conversation logs show your learning journey
